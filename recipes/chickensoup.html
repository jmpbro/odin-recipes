Chicken Soup Recipe

Cooked chicken soup is a timeless and universally loved dish, cherished for its comforting qualities, nourishing ingredients, and rich, homemade flavor. It is a warm, savory broth-based soup typically made by simmering tender pieces of chicken with a variety of vegetables, herbs, and seasonings. This simple yet deeply satisfying dish has roots in cuisines around the world and is often regarded as a remedy for colds, a symbol of comfort during difficult times, and a staple in family kitchens.

At its heart, chicken soup begins with a flavorful base. Most recipes start by gently sautéing aromatics such as chopped onions, garlic, and celery in a bit of oil or butter. Carrots are usually added for sweetness and color, creating a balanced trio of vegetables that serve as the foundation for the soup. Once the vegetables are softened and fragrant, chicken is added — often bone-in pieces like thighs or a whole chicken for deeper flavor — and everything is covered with water or chicken broth.

As the mixture simmers, the chicken slowly cooks and releases its juices, enriching the broth with flavor and nutrients. The gentle cooking process also allows the vegetables to become tender without losing their structure. Bay leaves, fresh herbs like thyme or parsley, and a pinch of black pepper contribute to the complexity of the soup. Salt is added carefully to enhance the natural flavors without overpowering them.

For the chicken broth

  • 200g uncooked chicken breast
  • 50g salted butter
  • 500ml good vegetable stock
  • 2 leeks
  • Pinch of salt and pepper

Method of cooking the chicken soup

  1. Wash and chop all vegetables (carrots, celery, onion, garlic).
  2. If using a whole chicken, rinse and cut into parts, or use bone-in thighs/drumsticks for easier prep.
  3. In a large soup pot or Dutch oven, heat 1–2 tablespoons of oil over medium heat
  4. Add chopped onion, carrots, and celery.
  5. Sauté for 5–7 minutes until softened and aromatic.
  6. Add garlic and cook for 1 more minute.
